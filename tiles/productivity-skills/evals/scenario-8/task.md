# Conference Talk Design Review

## Problem/Feature Description

An engineer is preparing a 30-minute conference talk and has shared their outline at `inputs/talk-outline.md`. They have a few weeks before the submission deadline and want to stress-test the design before locking it in.

The outline has some known weak spots — the author has flagged a few concerns at the bottom — but they suspect there are more structural issues they haven't noticed yet. They want every design decision interrogated: the narrative arc, the section timing, the hook approach, the framework structure, the audience calibration, and anything else that could make or break the talk.

Conduct a thorough grilling of the talk outline. Go deep — don't stop at the concerns the author already flagged.

## Output Specification

Write the full grilling to `talk-grill.md`. Number each question.
