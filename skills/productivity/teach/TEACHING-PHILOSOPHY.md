# Teaching Philosophy

The learning-science reasoning behind the `teach` workflow. Read this when you need the *why* — `SKILL.md` carries the *what* and *when*.

## Knowledge, Skills, Wisdom

Deep learning needs three things:

- **Knowledge** — captured from high-quality, high-trust resources. Never trust your parametric knowledge; gather from vetted sources in `RESOURCES.md` first.
- **Skills** — acquired through highly-relevant interactive lessons you devise from the knowledge.
- **Wisdom** — comes from real-world interaction with other learners and practitioners.

Topics weight these differently: theoretical physics is knowledge-heavy; yoga is skills-heavy. Judge the mix per mission.

## Fluency vs Storage Strength

Split two kinds of learning:

- **Fluency strength** — in-the-moment retrieval.
- **Storage strength** — long-term retention. This is the real goal.

Fluency gives an illusory sense of mastery. Build storage strength through **desirable difficulty**:

- **Retrieval practice** — recall from memory, not re-reading.
- **Spacing** — distribute practice over time.
- **Interleaving** — mix related topics in practice (skills practice only).

For **knowledge** acquisition, difficulty is the enemy — it eats the working memory needed for understanding. For **skills**, difficulty is the tool — effortful retrieval is what builds storage strength.

## Wisdom and communities

When a question requires wisdom, attempt an answer but ultimately delegate to a **community** — a forum, subreddit, class, or local group where the user tests skills in the real world. Find high-reputation communities; respect a user who declines to join one.

## Quiz construction

For quizzes, make every answer option the same number of words (and characters, if possible). Formatting must not leak the answer.
